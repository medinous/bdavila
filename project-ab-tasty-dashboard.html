AB Tasty Dashboard: a redesign project for CRO campaigns

AB Tasty is an all-inclusive B2B/SaaS tool for website and conversion rate optimization, personalization, user engagement, and A/B and MVT testing. Marketing, product and tech teams use AB Tasty’s AI-driven experimentation, personalization and product optimization platform to rapidly drive ROI and continuously deliver outstanding user experiences.

01/ Context

In the product team of AB Tasty we use the Spotify agile methodology. We work with chapters and squads. As a product designer, I am part of the *Design chapter* but also part of a transversal squad called *Product Experience,* responsible for the general user experience and delight of the product.

This project was the first one we built together as the *Product Experience* squad, so we knew that we had challenges ahead. For instance, right at the beginning we didn't have a dedicated Product Manager in our squad, which "forced" me to grasp some of the PM's responsibilities side by side with the tech lead throughout the project. The other challenge, but also exciting part of the project, is that the Dashboard is one of they key pages of the platform, where users see, manage, edit and create new content. And because none of the previous squads had any ownership on this project, the Dashboard has generated a huge design debt throughout the past years.

02/ Why we did this project

  • Technical debt: migration to React.js was necessary
  • Design debt: usability issues accumulated over the years due to lack of ownership

03/ Process

We planned a development cycle of 6 weeks, using sprints of 2 weeks (3 sprints in total). But the entire project took 16 weeks instead, due to some bumps on the way (COVID-19, less working hours, change of people on the team).

The design process started around two months before preparing the backlog for the 6-week cycle with the research phase, where I started gathering existing feedback from clients and conducting new interviews and usability testing with clients, Customer Success Managers and stakeholders.

04/ Main pain points found

  • Lack of consistency and clarity on the campaigns statuses: not clear when a test should be played (for the first time), paused or republished (when the test was running and you made changes that need to be updated)
  • Lack of consistency and clarity on the campaigns statuses: not clear when a test should be played (for the first time), paused or republished (when the test was running and you made changes that need to be updated)
  • Start and end dates of campaigns are not clear
  • The test type icons on the top left are misleading, they look like CTAs more than filters
  • It is confusing to see action buttons and static content all mixed up in the same components
  • Folders functionality is confusing. The folder icon/trigger appears in 3 different places on screen, with 3 different look & feel. The users engagement with this feature is very poor.
  • Clients want to delete tests from the folders and they can't
  • Test ID is very important and it's a common point of communication between the users, CSMs, TSEs, developers and QA. Currently is not very intuitive on how to see and copy the test ID.

05/ Solution phase

After framing the problem, I started creating different versions of mockups that were iterated and tested internally, using tools such as Sketch, Zeplin, InVision, Adobe XD and Loom (to record the interviews). I used Sketch to build the mockups, Zeplin to show the specifications to the development team, InVision to create the click-dummy prototype for the usability interviews and Adobe XD to generate animated mockups to explain interactions and transitions to developers.

Animation created with Adobe XD to show the transition between tabs

A new round of usability tests took place after the project was validated by the C-level board in order to define the final solution before starting the development cycle.

06/ Results

We planned a development cycle of 6 weeks, using sprints of 2 weeks (3 sprints in total). But the entire project took 16 weeks instead, due to some bumps on the way (COVID-19, less working hours, change of people on the team).

With this project, while addressing many user pain points accumulated over the past months, we were able to update the UI, increment small but smart UX improvements (without disrupting the current knowledge that users have from the tool, avoiding a big learning curve) and also launch new features.

  • We increased the general NPS score from 25 to 45 within 2 months after the release
  • We applied direct survey with clients, which showed a 73% of approval of the new solutions
  • Positive feedback from clients and CSMs for solving the main pain points addressed in item 05 above
  • Scalable UI to expand existing features and add new ones

07/ Challenges and Learnings

This is a very complex project and I am very happy to do it with a balanced team and a very structured process. It's the first time I am taking part on backlog prioritisation and Sprint planning, which means I am able to to contribute with the Design perspective on the process of project planning and management. For example, we added Design review as mandatory step on the workflow of developers and we added the research tasks as part of the project planning, delivering value and results at the end of every sprint
